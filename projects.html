    Projects

    Electronic Dropper

    I designed a mount, PCB and software for an electronic dropper post conversion for my Fox Transfer SL

    Light Sculpture

    For the CAD unit we made a Skateboard in onshape.

    The Deck

    I started out by making a fully constrained sketch using the center rectangle and circle tools. Then I extruded my sketch, put bolt holes in the board to mount the trucks and filleted the boards edge. At first I thought I needed to make circles in the sketch to extrude holdes but onshape has a specific hole tool that makes tolerances easy to set.

    The Trucks

    Making the trucks was very interesting to me because we made parts in the air before connecting them. One thing I learned is that you can use a sketch after you made it by using the use tool, this is very helpful because you don't need to remake constraints that you made before. I also helped Yannick with an issue where his sketch got constrained wrong because of one miss click and he got stuck.

    Wheels and Bearings

    The wheels and bearings were pretty simple, I revolved a sketch for the polyurethane part of the wheel and then made a bearing inside of the wheel using some of the dimensions with the use tool.

    Assembly

    I added bearings to both sides of the wheel then used a cool tool to combine everything. Its called replicate and it helps with repetitve assembly so that you dont have to go through all of the assembly steps every time.
